Teacher Briefing

B1 (Intermediate) Level

This class is heavily focused on emotional impact and identity. Peter Parker has been forgotten by the world and must start over.

Grammar Targets:

  • 🕰️ Used To: Discussing past states that are no longer true (He used to have friends).
  • ❤️ Causative Make: Expressing how things affect emotions (It makes him lonely).

Grammar Focus I

Past States (Used To)

We use USED TO to talk about habits, states, or situations that were true in the past, but are NOT true now.

The Past (Before)

"Peter used to have friends and a girlfriend."

The Present (Now)

"Now, he is completely alone."

Negative: He didn't use to be alone. (Note: no 'd' in use).

Grammar Focus II

Causative "Make"

We use MAKE + Object + Adjective/Verb to say that something causes an emotion or forces an action.

Creating an emotion (Adjective)

"Being alone makes him sad."

Causing an action (Base Verb)

"The responsibility makes him fight harder."
(Never use 'to': makes him to fight ❌)

Then vs Now

Rewrite the facts about Peter's life using Used to.

"He used to have a high-tech suit."

Fact: He didn't live alone. Now he lives in a cheap apartment.

"He didn't use to live alone."
